Reviews

Field-tested gear for EMT work, firecraft, travel, and recovery — measured against real scenarios.

Every item below is tested with repeatable drills (ignition timing, flow rates, boil tests, pack load balance). We explain the why — not just the specs — so you can pick what works in the field.

CAT-Style Tourniquet

★★★★☆ 4.5/5

Fast to apply one-handed with reliable occlusion. Windlass retention is secure; strap routing is intuitive under stress.

  • Pros
  • One-handed application
  • High occlusion success in drills
  • Good cold-weather handling
  • Cons
  • Bulkier than elastic TQs
  • Counterfeits are common — buy vetted

Verdict: Top choice for primary trauma kits. Practice until you can apply it in 20–25 seconds.

Related: EMT Kit Basics · Shock Recognition

Ferrocerium Rod (Large)

★★★★☆4.6/5

Throws consistent hot sparks; pairs well with fatwood curls and waxed jute. Durable in sub-freezing temps.

  • Pros
  • Excellent spark volume
  • Gloved-hand grip
  • Long service life
  • Cons
  • Heavier than micro rods

Verdict: Best “primary” striker for cold-weather firecraft drills.

Related: Cold-Weather Firecraft · Fire Readiness 101

Ultralight 45–55L Pack

★★★★☆4.2/5

Carries best with dense weight high and close to the spine. Side compression and load lifters make long days kinder on gait.

  • Pros
  • Efficient frame-to-hip transfer
  • Clean exterior (fewer snag points)
  • Good compression range
  • Cons
  • Less comfortable past ~35 lb
  • Requires thoughtful packing

Verdict: Ideal for 72-hour kits and lightweight travel systems.

Related: Pack Load Balance · Vagabonding Essentials

Hollow-Fiber Squeeze Filter

★★★★☆4.0/5

Fast flow on clear water; slows with silt. Backflush often; pair with chemicals in high-risk sources.

  • Pros
  • Lightweight, compact
  • Decent flow when new
  • Field-serviceable (backflush)
  • Cons
  • Prone to freezing damage
  • Clogs in silty sources

Verdict: Great first-line filter; add tablets/UV for redundancy.

Related: Water Disinfection 101

4-Season Canister Stove

★★★☆☆3.9/5

Excellent in shoulder seasons; needs careful canister warming and wind management in deep cold or at altitude.

  • Pros
  • Quick setup
  • Fine flame control
  • Compact pack size
  • Cons
  • Fuel performance dips in sub-freezing temps
  • Wind sensitivity without screen

Verdict: Good all-rounder; consider liquid fuel for extreme cold/altitude.
