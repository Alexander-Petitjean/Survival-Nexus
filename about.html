Mission

I’m Alexander Gordon Petitjean — a designer, maker, and problem-solver who turns lived experience into future-ready solutions. As a double below-knee amputee with a background in AI-assisted prototyping, outdoor survival education, and hands-on construction, I build tools and environments where humans can thrive — especially in unpredictable situations. Whether I’m designing an adaptive decking structure using GPT-augmented geometry, creating digital ecosystems for survival knowledge, or refining biomechanical efficiency one step at a time, I combine curiosity with resilience. I believe accessibility isn’t a constraint — it’s innovation waiting to happen. Welcome to my work: where creativity, technology, and survival intersect. We help people build self-reliance with field-tested knowledge and gear. Our focus spans EMT/trauma readiness, firecraft & shelter, treasure-hunting tools, and minimalist vagabond travel. We publish step-by-step guides, maintain a vetted supplier directory, and run transparent gear tests you can replicate.

Who We Are

Editor & Lead Tester

Outdoor generalist with a bias for evidence and repeatable experiments. Field background includes wilderness first aid drills, backcountry travel, tool repair, and lightweight systems. We actively seek feedback from emergency responders and experienced hobbyists.

Perspective Matters

Accessibility and inclusive design are core values. We consider different body types and abilities, and we note gear usability with gloved hands, cold-weather dexterity, one-handed operation, and weight-bearing constraints.

How We Test

Standard Protocol

  • Define use case: scenario, environment, failure modes.
  • Establish metrics: e.g., time-to-occlusion, strikes-to-ignite, runtime, durability cycles, mass/volume.
  • Run trials: minimum 3 trials per metric; note variance and conditions.
  • Document: photos, notes, and configuration details.
  • Publish: results with pros/cons and explicit trade-offs.

Ratings Are Explanations

Numbers without context mislead. We pair ratings with the “why” and call out edge cases where a lower-rated tool still wins for a specific job.
